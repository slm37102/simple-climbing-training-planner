## Lens A

### KG-A1 — No limiter diagnosis (P1, G1)

**The app cannot tell whether fingers, pulling, endurance, or technique is holding the athlete back.** With only 3 sessions/week, training a non-limiter is the most expensive possible mistake for G1: a V6 climber failing pumpy 7a needs a different plan than one with weak fingers on V6 crimps. This requires benchmark-vs-grade norm tables (e.g. "expected max hang %BW at 7a") that exist nowhere in the repo.

- *Pointers:* `js/storage.js:38-41` — `sportGrade`, `boulderGrade`, `dominantStyle`, `dominantAngle` are stored but read by **no prescription path** (the 2026-07 onboarding wizard now *writes* a target grade into `boulderGrade`/`sportGrade`, but nothing consumes them); `js/loads.js:38-46` — only `maxHang20mm`/`pullup1RM` are ever consumed; [`research/verified-findings.md`](research/verified-findings.md) § General — the 20 mm *measurement* is validated, its *interpretation* is missing.
- *Verdict:* close — highest-leverage missing coach knowledge. Research task first (KG-C6), then KG-D2.
- **DECIDED 2026-07-15 → [ADR-0011](adr/0011-limiter-readout.md).** Static three-line Settings card, target-grade anchored, informational-only: fingers vs the Advanced-tier norm band, pull-ups vs the diminishing-returns ceiling only, and the "limiter is likely elsewhere (technique/endurance — unmeasured)" inference when strength sits at norm — with the ~17% R² caveat in the card copy per `benchmark-norms.md`'s scope recommendation. Closes with KG-D2 when the card ships.
- **CLOSED 2026-07-17 → issue #50. KG-A1 + KG-D2 both close.** New `js/limiter.js` (`limiterReadout(benchmarks)`, pure function) compares `maxHang20mm` against the Lattice Advanced-tier norm band for the athlete's stored *target* `boulderGrade` (at/above → "fingers likely aren't your main limiter"; ≥1 grade-step below → "a limiter candidate"; in between → a softer "not yet a clear limiter candidate" middle reading not explicitly named by the ADR but needed for a defensible line whenever the athlete sits just under norm) and `pullup1RM` against the +65%-BW-added diminishing-returns ceiling (no grade table exists for pulling strength). An "elsewhere" line fires only when both strength lines are at/above norm. Every threshold is an app convention (KG-C7 posture) and the ~17%/~8–12% R² caveat ships in the card copy verbatim. Renders as a `data-limiter-card` on the Profile tab beside the Benchmarks card, recomputed on every render (so any benchmark change, including a retest save, refreshes it for free) — informational only, no prescription change. No schema change; `sw.js` SHELL/CACHE updated. 9 `[ADR-0011]` regression tests added to `tests/index.html` (178/178 green).

### KG-A2 — No intra-phase progressive overload (P1, G1)

**Every week of a phase prescribes the identical session.** An unchanging stimulus stops driving adaptation after ~2–3 weeks. Only the two weighted exercises auto-adjust (±5% off last RPE, `js/loads.js:22-27`); climbing volume, ARC duration, PE density, and limit-boulder difficulty never progress within a phase. On a 24-week cycle that means ~10 identical Base weeks.

- *Pointers:* `js/program.js` — `buildThuMain`/`buildSatMain` are static templates keyed only by (phase, flavor); the only week-level variation is the deload cut (`applyDeloadVolume`); verified-findings § Base (volume ramp then halve), § Strength (+2.5–10% when targets hit), § Power-endurance (rest cuts of 5 s/week).
- *Verdict:* close — write per-phase progression rules on paper first, then KG-D5.
- **CLOSED 2026-07-14 → [ADR-0009](adr/0009-intra-phase-progression.md).** Two of the three cited rules shipped: Base aerobic sessions (ARC, route pyramid) now ramp `prescribedTarget` ×1.1 per hard Base week (cap ×1.3, `applyBaseVolumeRamp`/`hardPhasePos` in `js/program.js`), composing with the existing deload cut into the verified ramp-then-halve shape; loaded exercises progress +2.5% on targets-hit (see KG-B5). The third rule — PE density rest-cuts — was deliberately **not** extended beyond the final 4 weeks: ADR-0006's verified band-1 design says "little density change" in the engine block, so KG-D5's rest-cut item is considered already satisfied by ADR-0006. Limit-boulder volume progression rejected (ADR-0001 posture). Base *boulder* Saturdays stay unramped while KG-B12 is open — the `energySystem` gate picks up a corrected session automatically.

### KG-A3 — No missed-session or life-interruption replanning (P1, G1+G2+G3)

**The plan is pure calendar math** — `Program.resolveDate` derives everything from the date; miss two weeks sick and the app acts as if the training happened. Hits all three goals: G2 (a hole in Build produces a mistimed taper), G3 (**load suggestions seed from previous-actual kg with no decay for elapsed time** — returning at full load after a layoff is a classic pulley-injury trigger, `js/loads.js:76-83`), G1 (lost adaptations untracked). Coach rules needed: miss ≤1 wk → resume; miss ≥2 wk in Base → extend Base/shift start; miss during Peak with a fixed comp date → decide which phase to sacrifice.

- *Pointers:* `js/program.js` — `resolveDate`/`effectiveStart`; `js/replan.js` (new) now reads `plan.days` to detect absence.
- *Verdict:* close — high frequency in real life for a working adult training 3×/week. Feeds KG-D3.
- **CLOSED 2026-07-08 → [ADR-0008](adr/0008-missed-session-replanning.md).** Implemented the "≤1 wk / ≥2 wk" split mechanically: `Replan.detectGap` flags a gap once a main-slot (Mon/Thu/Sat) day has gone unlogged for ≥8 days (soft, informational) or ≥14 days (major); the Today-tab banner offers a whole-week "extend plan" lever (`settings.scheduleShiftDays`, `startDate` mode only) that pushes `effectiveStart` forward and re-snaps to Monday. The G3 load-seeding risk this gap named directly is fixed too: `Loads.layoffDecay` now cuts a stale previous-actual seed up to 15% by ~5 weeks off before it ever reaches `autoAdjust`. The third rule — "miss during Peak with a fixed comp date → decide which phase to sacrifice" — was deliberately **not** automated: in `compDate` mode the banner is informational only (goal date can't move), leaving that judgment call to the athlete via Profile, the same posture ADR-0007 took on taper length.

### KG-A4 — No monitoring model (P1, G3+G1)

**Nothing defines what signals say the plan is or isn't working.** The app logs readiness and RPE and retests once per Base block, but has no decision rules: no "readiness trending down 2 weeks → pull volume" (overreach flag), no "retest flat two cycles → change stimulus" (plateau), no finger/pulley pain check-in distinct from generic soreness. Monitoring is the cheapest coach function and it feeds every other gap.

- *Pointers:* `js/loads.js:10-18` — the readiness→multiplier mapping is the *only* signal consumer, and it looks at a single day; `js/views/log.js` charts display trends but interpret nothing; verified-findings § Deload (fitness-minus-fatigue rationale).
- *Verdict:* close — define 3–5 signals + thresholds + responses on paper first, then KG-D6.
- **DECIDED 2026-07-16 → [ADR-0014](adr/0014-monitoring-model.md)** (evidence sweep: [`research/monitoring-signals-evidence.md`](research/monitoring-signals-evidence.md)). Four exception-catcher signals — readiness trend (7-day vs 28-day baseline), RPE drift at constant load, retest trajectory (needs the new benchmark history), Silbernagel pain check-in — advisory flags with one-tap accept, Log-tab panel + Today banner, one schema bump. ACWR/sRPE/HRV rejected on the sweep's disqualifiers. Closes with KG-D6 when the signals ship.
- **CLOSED 2026-07-17 → issue #52. KG-A4 + KG-D6 both close.** New `js/monitoring.js` exports the four signals as pure functions of stored history (`computeSignals({days, benchmarkHistory, todayPain, asOfIso})`) — readiness trend (7-day vs 28-day avg, ≥0.5pt drop), RPE drift (2 consecutive load-matched hangboard/pullup sessions over target RPE at non-increased kg — needs the logged exercise to carry its own `rpeRange`, now persisted via `today.js`'s `getOrInitDay`), retest trajectory (2 flat-or-down retests from the new `globalBenchmarks.history`), and the Silbernagel pain check-in (0–10 finger/elbow + "settled by morning?", green/amber/red). **Schema v5→v6:** `globalBenchmarks.history` array (appended by the new `Storage.saveRetestBenchmarks`, used by every retest-save path instead of the plain overwrite `setGlobalBenchmarks`); daily readiness history needed no new field (`plan.days[date].readiness` already persists per day — the gap was nothing reading it back, not storage); a `pain` sub-field on `readiness`. Advisory + one-tap accept (ADR-0008 idiom): a Today-tab banner (real-current-date only, matching the gap-banner precedent) and a Log-tab panel, both dismissible per-day; only the readiness-trend signal has a real mutation on accept — an athlete-triggered early volume cut for the coming 7 days (`settings.earlyVolumeCuts` date ranges, applied via the existing `applyDeloadVolume` mechanism as a `prescribeForContext` post-processing step, deliberately not touching the phase-pattern's deload/retest flags; the 2026-07-17 post-review fix pass switched this from a current-weekIdx cut to the date-range form to match the ADR's "coming week's sessions", and made the other three signals' responses actually render — pain-red links `return-from-tweak.md`, retest-plateau links `end-of-cycle-review.md`, amber holds the ADR-0009 progression via `Loads.resolveEffective`'s `holdProgression`). 12 `[ADR-0014]` regression tests added to `tests/index.html` (199/199 green), including a schema-migration idempotency test and a `mergeRemote` round-trip for the new history array. `sw.js` SHELL/CACHE updated.

### KG-A5 — Taper knowledge is a stub (P2, G2)

**G2 is literally "peak for a date" and the taper is the mechanism — but the app's taper is 1–2 fixed weeks of static light sessions.** Research already verified in-repo says: cut volume 60–90% while holding intensity, a *progressive* taper beats step/linear, length scales 4–28 days with event type, and strength is maintained with a touch every 5–10 days. None of that is modeled.

- *Pointers:* `js/program.js` — `buildPhasePattern` (taper = 1–2 weeks by formula) and the taper session templates; verified-findings § Peaking & tapering.
- *Verdict:* close — the evidence is already verified; this is a translation gap. Feeds KG-D4.
- **DECIDED 2026-07-02 → [ADR-0007](adr/0007-taper-hold-intensity-peaktype.md); IMPLEMENTED 2026-07-04.** Hold intensity, step-cut volume (`applyTaperVolume`), mandatory rest day before the goal (`rest-pre-goal` at `totalDays − 2`), + a `settings.peakType` length lever (comp 1 wk / trip 2 / project 2). The load-bearing correction was removing the intensity drop (shipped 2026-07-03); progressive decay was rejected (evidence is for ≥10-day tapers). `peakType` shipped **without** a schema bump — `defaultSettings()` shallow-merge backfills it per the CLAUDE.md convention.

### KG-A6 — Power-endurance dosing and placement unresolved (P2, G2+G3)

**The app's PE exposure matches neither of its two sources.** Hörst: dedicate the last 2–4 weeks to anaerobic-lactic work and warns longer PE risks overtraining (G3). Lattice: an 8-week PE block, 2×/week, with a mid-block rest week and rest-cuts in the final 4 weeks. In hybrid mode the app delivers 4×4s roughly every other week (flavor alternation, `js/program.js:113-115`) spread across Build+Peak with no density progression and no anchoring to the peak date — under-dosed by Lattice's standard, mis-sequenced by Hörst's.

- *Pointers:* `js/program.js` — `buildThuMain`/`buildSatMain` build-phase sport sessions; verified-findings § Power-endurance, § Peaking. The two sources may describe different energy systems — adjudicate KG-C5 first.
- *Verdict:* close after KG-C5.
- **DECIDED 2026-07-02 → [ADR-0006](adr/0006-power-endurance-two-band-model.md); IMPLEMENTED 2026-07-04.** KG-C5 taxonomy settled (two energy-system bands, not a conflict). Shipped: Build sport Thu = 60/60 threshold intervals RPE 7–8.5 (band 1); Peak sport Thu = 30/30 lactic RPE 9.5–10 (band 2, confined to the final ≤4 weeks); interval rest tightens 5s/week inside the final 4 weeks (`densityRest`, clamped ≥2:30); Sat triples/4×4 are single-system with the open-climb cool-down optional. ≤1 lactic session/wk holds by construction (band 2 lives on Thu only).

### KG-A8 — No inter-cycle progression model (P2, G1)

**Nothing defines what changes in cycle N+1.** V5→V7 is a multi-cycle, 1–2+ year project; retests reset the kg math but the program itself is identical every cycle — same protocols, same edges, same session library. Needed: when to rotate protocols, when to shift phase ratios toward the evolving limiter, and when to ratchet Peak aggressiveness back up (the [training-philosophy open question](training-philosophy.md#peer-reviewed-support-and-gaps) — which becomes live exactly when the athlete succeeds at G1).

- *Verdict:* close as a written end-of-cycle review checklist first (KG-D7); automation was tracked as the remaining half.
- **Checklist half shipped 2026-07-16** → [`end-of-cycle-review.md`](end-of-cycle-review.md) (issue [#43](https://github.com/slm37102/simple-climbing-training-planner/issues/43), closed KG-D7). Covers retest trajectory (incl. what two flat retests in a row means — a judgment call), which protocols to rotate, when to shift phase ratios toward the evolving limiter, and the Peak-aggressiveness ratchet-up question.
- **Adjacent, resolved 2026-07-17 → issue #51 (ADR-0012).** Benchmarks also go stale *within* a cycle (only retest closes Base at week 6). Both a post-goal retest slot and a staleness-gated Build-Monday micro-retest ship; that answered benchmark freshness, not the inter-cycle *model*.
- **Automation half RULED WON'T-FIX 2026-07-23; reminder-nudge slice shipped instead.** The remaining scope was "auto-detect drift/limiter shift and auto-actuate cycle N+1's parameters, no human read required." Ruled won't-fix on correctness/safety grounds (independent of build effort — see CLAUDE.md's "Weighing work" note):
  1. **The high-value detection half is already automated.** `retestTrajectorySignal` (`js/monitoring.js`, ADR-0014) already fires on "two flat cycles in a row" and links this checklist; `limiterReadout` (`js/limiter.js`, ADR-0011) already surfaces the likely limiter. What's left is only the decision + actuation.
  2. **The decision rules are explicitly unvalidated.** The checklist itself flags every rotation/ratio/ratchet rule as "judgment call, not a cited threshold" with no RCT. Auto-actuating them would bake uncited convention into behaviour — the exact KG-C7 anti-pattern the project just spent effort *disclosing away from*.
  3. **The inputs are human-only.** "Was the dip illness or life-load?", "did Peak feel undertrained?", "any near-miss/tweak?" aren't captured by the app; the limiter signal explains only ~17% of grade variance (a sanity check, not a diagnosis), too weak to auto-drive a phase-ratio shift.
  4. **Injury-risk asymmetry (G3 gates G1/G2).** Auto-ratcheting Peak aggressiveness is precisely the lever [ADR-0001](adr/0001-soften-peak-phase-for-intermediate-athlete.md) softened for injury-aversion; a wrong automated ratchet costs a whole cycle. Durability outranks progression by `project-goals.md`'s stated trade-off order.
  5. **"Simple over clever."** A once-per-cycle decision engine is large ongoing surface area that doesn't demonstrably beat the athlete reading a checklist with the Log tab open — in a single-user app where the athlete *is* the developer.
- **Shipped slice (the one real gap):** nothing *nudged* the athlete to run the checklist at cycle end (the retest-plateau signal only fires after two flat cycles — late by design). A reminder now sits on the Cycle-Complete card (`cycleCompleteHtml` in `js/views/today.js`), above "Start New Cycle", linking `end-of-cycle-review.md` — a nudge, not an engine, and it actuates no decision. `[KG-A8 nudge]` regression test in `tests/cases/02-today-view.js`; `sw.js` CACHE bump.

### KG-A10 — Style/weakness individualization unused (P3, G1)

`dominantStyle`/`dominantAngle` are collected (defaults: crimp, slight-overhang) and ignored; a crimp-dominant athlete progresses fastest with anti-style exposure in Base, but the session library is identical for every profile. This is the actuation half of KG-A1.

- *Verdict:* close cheaply — bias session *text* by stored style ("include 2 anti-style problems"); full auto-programming out of scope.
- **CLOSED 2026-07-16 (issue #41).** Base/Build boulder-flavor Thursday and Saturday sessions now carry a session-level `styleNote` anti-style cue, e.g. the default crimp/slight-overhang profile reads "include 2 anti-style problems — slopers/pinches, slab or vertical". A new `buildAntiStyleCue(dominantStyle, dominantAngle)` maps each stored style/angle to its opposite via two small lookup tables — grip styles pair by mechanics (crimp/pocket = closed-chain discrete-hold grips vs. sloper/pinch = open-hand/thumb-opposition grips), angles split by steepness (slab/vert vs. slight-overhang/steep/roof) — and returns `null` for unset/unrecognised values, so the note is omitted entirely rather than guessed. `prescribeForContext` attaches the cue (via `attachStyleNote`) *after* building the session, gated on `slot` (`thu-main`/`sat-main`) + `resolvedFlavor === 'boulder'` + `phase` (`base`/`build`) — deliberately keyed on phase/flavor/slot rather than sessionId, so it keeps applying even if a phase's Thu/Sat template is later split or renamed (mirrors how `applyDeloadVolume`/`applyBaseVolumeRamp`/`applyTaperVolume` already attach their own session-level notes post-build, rather than baking them into `buildThuMain`/`buildSatMain`'s branches). `benchmarks` threads as an optional 3rd argument through `Program.build` → `prescribeForContext` (defaults to `null` — no cue — so every pre-existing caller that doesn't pass it is unaffected); `js/views/today.js`, `js/views/calendar.js`, and `js/views/profile.js` now pass `Storage.get().benchmarks`, and `today.js`'s header renders the cue in its own line alongside the existing deload/ramp/taper note slot. Peak, Taper, and sport-flavor sessions are untouched by construction (the gate excludes them). Text only, per the recorded verdict — no new UI, no schema change, no automated problem selection. `[KG-A10]` regression tests added to `tests/index.html` (120/120 green) covering the default profile, a different explicit profile, omitted/empty/unset/unrecognised fields (no cue), and confirming Peak/Taper/sport sessions stay untouched. Closes KG-D8.

### KG-A14 — Readiness autoregulation never touches climbing sessions (P2, G3+G1)

**The readiness check-in only modulates hangboard/pull-up kg — the highest-injury sessions get zero readiness modulation.** The readiness multiplier (×0.85 / ×1.0 / ×1.05 / suggest-rest) feeds only `Loads.resolveEffective`, which computes kg for `hangboard`/`pullup` kinds alone. The 30/30 lactic intervals at RPE 9.5–10, limit bouldering, and 4×4s — exactly where autoregulation matters most, because intensity is maximal and load is unquantified — run unmodified on a "Lighter" day, and "suggest rest" suppresses a kg number while leaving the full climbing session prescribed. Surfaced by the 2026-07-14 external coaching review ([`coach-review.md`](coach-review.md), W5, `[new]`) — opened late (2026-07-15): the review batch's ID pass counted five untracked findings, but W5 was a sixth.
