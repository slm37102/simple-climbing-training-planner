# Climbing-kind exercise prescriptions — published guidance vs the planner

> **What this is.** Reference research for the "climbing-kind" exercises the planner prescribes — bouldering (projecting / flash / limit), route climbing (pyramid / mileage / redpoint), circuit & interval formats (4×4 boulder triples, 60/60 and 30/30 route intervals), ARC training, open-climbing mileage, and campus-board work. It gathers what Lattice Training, Eric Hörst (*Training for Climbing*, 3rd ed.), the Anderson brothers (*Rock Climber's Training Manual*), and peer-reviewed sport science publish for the **execution and volume** of each, then cross-references the current app prescriptions.
>
> **Scope.** Written for this planner's one athlete: an intermediate climber, V5–V6 boulder / ~7a lead, ~3 years' experience, training ~3×/week, both bouldering and sport. Prescriptions are deliberately biased toward that profile and softened for injury risk where relevant (ADR-0001).
>
> **This is reference material, not app behaviour.** It describes what the literature says and where the code agrees or diverges — it does not change any prescription. The numbers here are **evidence-based starting points to adjust, not laws — and none of it is medical advice.**
>
> **Highest-trust local source first.** Most claims below are already in [`verified-findings.md`](verified-findings.md) (a 3-vote adversarially fact-checked corpus). Where that corpus and the earlier single-pass [`deep-research-report.md`](deep-research-report.md) disagree, the verified corpus wins; numbers that live *only* in the deep-research report are labelled **lower-trust**.

## Evidence-tier headline (read this before the table)

For these **climbing-kind** exercises, **Tier 1 (controlled trials) is essentially empty.** The peer-reviewed climbing trials in our corpus — López-Rivera & González-Badillo 2019 (PMID 30988852), Mundry 2021 (PMID 34188125), Gilmore 2024 (PMID 39560837) — study **hangboard / finger strength**, *not* 4×4s, limit bouldering, ARC volume, route intervals, or campusing. They must **not** be cited to prop up the exercises on this page. The only peer-reviewed touch-points are *correlational* (e.g. campus-board score correlates with finger strength, r=0.82, EJAP 2025) — associations, not training trials.

Consequently **almost every prescription below is Tier 2 (coaching consensus: Lattice / Hörst / Anderson and allied coaching sources)**, with a handful of genuine **Tier 3 (open questions)** — chiefly the exact boulder-volume counts, the route "pyramid 4-3-2-1" framing, and the ARC "%effort" figure. Saying so is a finding, not a gap.

## Per-exercise detail

### Boulder (projecting / flash / submaximal) — lines 1–4

**Published guidance.**
- **Volume-vs-power split.** Coaching consensus separates *limit/power* bouldering (few, maximal, fully-rested) from *submaximal volume* bouldering (many, sub-limit, for skill and capacity). Submaximal-volume sessions are climbed on easy problems at low effort — one source cites ~30 boulders in an hour at ≤5/10 effort ([Climbing.com / TrainingBeta volume workouts]) — while flash-level "medium" work sits around 70% of climbing near flash grade.
- **Phase mapping.** Sub-limit power-volume (a grade or two below max) belongs to **build** (converting strength into movement) and **base** (skill + capacity); pure fun/submaximal mileage belongs to **taper** (stay sharp, shed fatigue). The taper principle from Lattice is explicit: *short, intense sessions to stay well-recruited and well-rested; don't overdo it and carry fatigue into send attempts* (`verified-findings.md`, Peaking & tapering).

**Evidence tier.** Tier 2 (coaching consensus) for the *concept* of separating volume from limit work and for phase placement. **Tier 3** for the exact problem counts (6–10, 3–5) and durations — no published source pins these numbers; they are athlete-scaled author judgment.

**Cross-reference.**
- **Line 1 (projecting, base) — PARTIAL.** 60–90 min is consistent with limit/volume session lengths (limit sessions run 60–75 min; `verified-findings.md`). Calling it "4×4-style projecting" blends two distinct ideas (projecting ≠ 4×4 laps); the effort band RPE 7.5–9 is a reasonable base capacity/skill target. Problem count is unspecified — acceptable for an open session.
- **Line 2 (flash-grade, taper) — PARTIAL.** "Below max · long rest" at RPE 7–8.5 correctly expresses the taper's stay-fresh intent. The specific "6–10 problems" count is **author judgment (Tier 3)** — no source publishes it.
- **Line 3 (flash/onsight, build) — PARTIAL.** "1 grade below max" for movement-quality power-volume is consensus; "3–5 problems" is **author judgment (Tier 3)**.
- **Line 4 (fun submaximal, taper) — PARTIAL.** "2–3 grades below max · no projecting" at RPE 6–7.5 matches both the taper-freshness principle and the submaximal-mileage intensity band (2–3 grades below max onsight; `verified-findings.md`, ARC section). Duration author-set but reasonable.

### Limit bouldering — lines 5–7

**Published guidance (well-covered in the corpus).**
- **Move length: no more than 3–5 moves** (six or fewer hand moves); beyond that it becomes power-endurance, not power (Hörst; TrainingBeta — `verified-findings.md`, Power phase, votes 3-0).
- **Rest 3–5 min between goes** so every attempt is full-power; after a genuine all-out go you may need **5 min or more** (Hörst; TrainingBeta 3-0).
- **Effort ≈ 100%** — one quality all-out go beats many moderate attempts (TrainingBeta 3-0).
- **Session length 60–75 min**; **stop when power output drops** — "fatigue has no place in limit bouldering" (Hörst 3-0 / 2-1).
- **Frequency ≤ 1–2×/week**, aggregate hard climbing ≤ 3–4 days/week; never limit-boulder two days running.

**Evidence tier.** Tier 2, but unusually strong and consistent across Hörst, TrainingBeta, and Climbing.com. Tier 1 empty (no controlled limit-bouldering trial).

**Cross-reference.**
- **Line 5 (build) — MATCH.** 3–5 problems, 3–5 attempts, 3–5 min rest are verbatim consensus. RPE 8.5–9.5 is a deliberately *build-phase* (slightly sub-100%) version of limit work — sensible for the athlete. Caveat: the "**2–3 sets**" figure appears only in the **lower-trust** `deep-research-report.md` ("No specific citation, but this is standard practice") — treat as coaching-plausible, not verified.
- **Line 6 (peak) — MATCH.** The strongest match on the page: 3–5 move sequences, 4–8 attempts, 3–5 min rest, **stop when power drops (~20–30 min)**, RPE 9–9.5 — every element is verbatim in the corpus.
- **Line 7 (project attempts) — MATCH.** "Max 5 quality attempts · 5+ min rest" directly matches "rest 5 min or more after all-out efforts" and "quality over quantity."

### Route climbing (pyramid / redpoint / mileage) — lines 8–11

**Published guidance.**
- **Redpoint pyramids** *are* a published concept (Anderson RCTM "Rock Prodigy" and Hörst both use grade-pyramids as a base/performance structure) — but neither the free RCTM redpoint page nor the corpus states a specific **"4-3-2-1 routes climbed back to back, 1 grade below redpoint"** base session. That exact framing (a fixed 4-3-2-1 count, back-to-back, in the *base* phase) is **author synthesis**.
- **Mileage / aerobic route work** at **~2 grades below redpoint threshold** keeps effort aerobic (`verified-findings.md`, ARC/aerobic section, Hörst 3-0). Lattice's aerobic-power emphasis is "the real anti-pump key for route climbers."
- **Redpoint/peak goes:** peak = prioritize freshness — *the closer to sending, the more rest between attempts and the lower the session volume* (`verified-findings.md`, Peaking; Bechtel/Lattice 3-0). A performance peak yields roughly **2–3 hard sends over ~1 month**.
- Related corpus protocol (not the same as the app's): **Lattice double route laps** — climb, lower, immediately re-climb; 5 sets, ≥10 min rest, at 1–2 grades under onsight (power-endurance, not redpoint).

**Evidence tier.** Tier 2 for mileage intensity and peak-freshness. **Tier 3** for the pyramid 4-3-2-1 base framing and the exact redpoint attempt counts.

**Cross-reference.**
- **Line 8 (pyramid, base) — PARTIAL.** Pyramid concept published; "4-3-2-1 back-to-back at 1 below redpoint in base" is author synthesis. "Back to back" also sits oddly against typical pyramids (which rest between routes). RPE 7.5–9 reasonable for a base capacity session.
- **Line 9 (redpoint, peak) — PARTIAL.** "2–4 quality goes · 20+ min rest" aligns well with the peak-freshness principle; the exact numbers are author judgment. The generous 20+ min rest is appropriately conservative for peak.
- **Line 10 (project/redpoint, taper) — PARTIAL.** "2–3 quality goes" matches "2–3 hard sends per peak"; count is author-set but consistent.
- **Line 11 (mileage) — PARTIAL.** "1–2 grades below redpoint · walking rest" at RPE 7–8.5 matches aerobic-mileage intensity guidance; "6–10 routes" is author judgment (Tier 3).

### Circuit — 60/60 and 30/30 route intervals — lines 12–13

**Published guidance (verbatim in corpus).**
- **60/60 (threshold / aerobic-power):** 60s moderately-hard climbing / 60s rest (1:1), **10–30 min aggregate**, target **RPE 7–8.5**, stop before the "major pump zone of 8.5+" (Hörst, `verified-findings.md`, PE section, 3-0). Build-phase adaptation.
- **30/30 (lactic / anaerobic-capacity):** 30s all-out (RPE 9–10) / 30s rest, **×6 = one set**, rest 10–15 min, **up to 4 sets** for well-conditioned climbers, deep pump expected (Climbing.com / Treadwall, 3-0). Peak-phase adaptation.

**Evidence tier.** Tier 2, but among the best-specified protocols in climbing coaching (consistent Hörst + Treadwall + Climbing.com numbers). Tier 1 empty.

**Cross-reference.**
- **Line 12 (60/60, build) — MATCH.** 1:1 ratio, 10–30 min, RPE 7–8.5, "never above 8.5" — all verbatim.
- **Line 13 (30/30, peak) — MATCH.** ×6 = set and RPE 9.5–10 match. The app's **"2–3 sets" is *more conservative*** than the published "up to 4 sets" — an appropriate injury-risk softening for this athlete, not a divergence to fix.

### Circuit — 4×4 (boulder triples & route/link circuits) — lines 14–15

**Published guidance.**
- **Classic 4×4 (anaerobic capacity):** 4 boulder problems set **2–4 grades below max** (e.g. a V8 climber picks V5s), each lapped 4× back-to-back = 16 ascents, ~1:1 work:rest between laps, 5–10 min between problems (Climbing.com, `verified-findings.md`, 3-0). **Two PE sessions/week.**
- **Treadwall 4×4:** 4 sets of four 30-on/30-off climbs, 4–5 min between sets (3-0).
- **Route/link circuits (Lattice sectioned circuit):** break a climb at/slightly-above redpoint into 4 sections, rest 50% of each section's climb time, all 4 sections = 1 set, 2–4 sets (3-0).

**Evidence tier.** Tier 2, strong and consistent for structure; the *grade/intensity* targets are what the app diverges on.

**Cross-reference.**
- **Line 14 (boulder triples 4×4) — PARTIAL.** Structure (4 boulders, 3–4 sets, back-to-back) fits the 4×4 family. **The grade target diverges:** the app prescribes **"1–2 grades below max"** where the published 4×4 uses **"2–4 grades below max."** The app runs its 4×4 *harder* than published — consistent with RPE 8.5–9.5, but note that none of the app's three 4×4 flavours actually hits the classic 2–4-below-max target. This is a genuine, defensible divergence (biases toward power-endurance-at-higher-intensity), not an error, but worth flagging.
- **Line 15 (4×4 circuits, build) — PARTIAL.** "4 routes/links · 3 sets" fits the 4×4 / sectioned-circuit set count. "50–60% redpoint" intensity is a plausible power-endurance target but is **not verbatim** in the corpus (Lattice's sectioned circuit is set *at or slightly above* redpoint; the 50–60% figure is closer to a capacity/threshold circuit). Coaching-plausible; call it consensus-adjacent author judgment.

### ARC (Aerobic Restoration & Capillarity) — line 16

**Published guidance (extensively covered in corpus).**
- **Structure:** 2–3 sets of 20–45 min continuous climbing (commonly **two or three 30-min sets, 10 min rest between**); many plans prescribe **90+ min total** per workout; 2–3 sessions/week (`verified-findings.md`, Base section — multiple sources, several 3-0).
- **Intensity: very low — 10–40% of max effort / 3–4 number grades below limit / ~2 grades below redpoint threshold**, maintaining a mild sustained pump, never a strong pump, never occluding blood flow.
- **Adaptation:** capillary density / aerobic base — base phase; needs ≥6–8 weeks for significant effect.

**Evidence tier.** Tier 2, very well corroborated across RCTM, Uphill Athlete, Hörst, and others.

**Cross-reference.**
- **Line 16 (arc, base) — PARTIAL.** The **structure matches** ("2 × 30 min" and "OR 90 min easy laps" both align with published 30-min sets / 90-min totals) and **RPE 4–6 matches** the very-low-intensity requirement. The outlier is **"60–70% effort":** published ARC intensity is **10–40% of max** (or 3–4 grades below limit). As written, 60–70% is higher than any published ARC figure. It *may* be intended as "% of redpoint" (ambiguous), which would be closer to defensible — but on its face it contradicts the source. Flag the "%effort" number specifically; the rest of the line is well-grounded.

### Open-climb (mileage / technique) — lines 17–18

**Published guidance.** No dedicated "open-climb" protocol exists in the literature; it maps to two published ideas: **easy mileage for skill/technique** (Hörst's technique emphasis) and **low-intensity aerobic mileage** (ARC-adjacent, well below max). Both are base/recovery-flavoured.

**Evidence tier.** Tier 2 for the concept (mileage builds skill and aerobic base); **Tier 3** for the specific durations.

**Cross-reference.**
- **Line 17 (technique) — PARTIAL.** 30–45 min submax mileage at RPE 6–7.5 is a reasonable skill/technique dose; duration author-set.
- **Line 18 (easy, optional) — PARTIAL.** 45–90 min "well below max" at RPE 4–6 reads as low-intensity aerobic mileage; intensity and volume sit inside ARC-family norms, so this is well-aligned with published aerobic-base guidance.

### Campus board — lines 19–20

**Published guidance (corpus-covered).**
- **Readiness gate (verbatim):** 2–5 yrs consistent climbing, **V5–V6 / 5.12, 15–20 solid pull-ups, and a 1-2-3-4-5 ladder without matching hands** (`verified-findings.md`, Power phase, 3-0). "If you cannot do a 1-2-3-4-5 ladder without matching, campusing is NOT yet recommended."
- **Execution:** 2–3 movement types (max ladders, double dynos, up-downs) on the biggest rungs ("Big Rungs + Big Moves = More Power"); minimize contact time on the lower rung (plyometric intent).
- **Volume/rest:** intermediate entry **"2-2-2"** (2 sets × 2 reps, 2 min rest); rest **3–5 min** for power work; 3–6 reps per exercise then 2–5 min rest.
- **Frequency:** start 1×/week → build to 2×/week; ≤3×/week even for elites; run in 3–6-week phases, not year-round. Highly strenuous — skip on any finger tweak.

**Evidence tier.** Tier 2 (Climbing.com, Gripped, Power Company). One peer-reviewed correlational touch-point: campus score correlates with finger strength (r=0.82, EJAP 2025) — association only.

**Cross-reference.**
- **Line 19 (build warmup) — PARTIAL.** "2–3 ladders × 5 min rest" — the 5-min rest fits the 3–5 min window. Using campus as a *submaximal warmup* (RPE 7–8) is author framing, not a published protocol, but is a defensible low-dose priming use given the gate is met.
- **Line 20 (peak) — MATCH.** The **readiness gate (15–20 pull-ups + 1-2-3-4-5 ladder without matching) and the 3–5 min rest are verbatim** in the corpus. "2–3 attempts × 2 sets" is close to the intermediate "2-2-2" entry and inside the 3–6-rep window. RPE 8.5–9 and "skip on any finger tweak" match campus's high-strain, quality-over-fatigue framing. Well-grounded.

## Bottom line

The **interval protocols (60/60, 30/30), limit bouldering, and the campus readiness gate are the best-grounded prescriptions** — their numbers are verbatim coaching consensus in the fact-checked corpus, and the app is if anything slightly *more conservative* than published (fewer 30/30 sets, sub-100% build-limit RPE). The **weakest-grounded** are the **exact boulder-volume counts** (6–10, 3–5 problems — author judgment, Tier 3), the **route "pyramid 4-3-2-1" base framing** (the pyramid concept is real but the specific back-to-back base session is author synthesis), and two genuine **divergences from published numbers**: the **4×4 grade target** (app "1–2 below max" vs published "2–4 below max" — the app runs it harder) and the **ARC "60–70% effort"** figure (published ARC is 10–40% of max). Across all climbing-kind exercises, **Tier 1 controlled-trial evidence is essentially absent** — the finger-strength/antagonist RCTs in the corpus do not test these formats, so everything here rests on coaching consensus, which the planner follows faithfully except at the flagged points.
