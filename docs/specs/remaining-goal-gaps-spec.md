# Spec: closing the plan's remaining goal-gaps (G1 progression / G2 peaking / G3 durability)

**Status:** Draft — not yet locked, not yet published to the issue tracker. This is a vision/roadmap document, not a single agent-implementable ticket; it is intended to be sliced into individual tickets (see Further Notes) rather than implemented as one PR.

**Inputs:** [`project-goals.md`](../project-goals.md) (G1/G2/G3 definitions), [`knowledge-gaps.md`](../knowledge-gaps.md) (gap IDs, verdicts, status), [`coach-review.md`](../coach-review.md) (2026-07-14 external audit, W-numbered findings), [ADR-0009](../adr/0009-intra-phase-progression.md) (the most recent gap closure, for precedent on scope/format).

## Problem Statement

The athlete's stated goals are G1 (reach V7 boulder / 7b sport), G2 (arrive at a chosen date at cycle-best form), and G3 (train 3×/week consistently, injury-free). The app's architecture — periodized macrocycle, phase-shaped hangboard protocols, deload/taper mechanics, autoregulation — is sound and evidence-audited (332 verified claims, zero unresolved conflicts, per `research/README.md`). ADR-0009 just closed the single biggest known cap on G1 (no progressive overload within a phase). But the 2026-07-14 coaching review and the knowledge-gaps ledger both show the plan still falls short of fully serving its own three goals in identifiable, named ways:

- **G1 is capped** by hybrid-mode dose-halving (every quality trained by weekly flavor alternation lands at half frequency), by the absence of any limiter diagnosis (the app trains qualities it cannot confirm are the actual bottleneck), and by two mechanical bugs that quietly distort the training stimulus (a fake retest-week deload, a taper volume double-cut).
- **G2 is under-served** by a peak-type lever that only changes taper *length*, not the specificity of the goal-day rehearsal itself (a comp peak trains identically to a trip/project peak).
- **G3's residual risk** is under-dosed antagonist/injury-prevention work (1×/week, dropped entirely on deload weeks) and the complete absence of a monitoring model — nothing watches trends (readiness sliding, RPE drifting, retests going flat) to catch overreach before it becomes an injury.

Little of this is unknown to the project — most items below already have a stable `KG-*` ID and a recorded verdict in `knowledge-gaps.md` (W5 got its ID, KG-A14, during the 2026-07-15 intake audit; W12 is KG-B11), and the rest are named findings from the coaching review (W13, the Sunday-stacking note) tracked inside existing entries rather than under their own IDs. What doesn't yet exist is a single document that organizes the *remaining* work by which goal it serves, separates what's ready to build from what still needs a design decision, and gives the athlete one place to decide sequencing.

## Solution

Close every currently-open Lens A/B knowledge gap that isn't explicitly Won't-fix/Accepted, **plus the untracked (`[new]`) coach-review findings that don't yet have a KG ID but serve the same three goals**, organized under the goal (G1/G2/G3) each serves. For gaps with an already-recorded, concrete verdict, this document *is* the implementation decision — no further adjudication needed before a ticket can be cut. For gaps whose verdict explicitly calls for a design decision "on paper first" (monitoring signals, hybrid-mode restructuring, the limiter-diagnosis readout), and for untracked coach findings with no recorded verdict at all, this document states the problem and the direction, and calls out — rather than invents — the missing decision, so a follow-up ADR/mini-spec can be scoped precisely instead of retrofitted around code someone already wrote. A handful of coach-review findings are deliberately left out entirely; those are named in Out of Scope so the omission is visible rather than silent.

Two existing seams carry almost all of this work (see Testing Decisions): `Program.build(plan, dateISO)` for every change to *what* gets prescribed, and `Loads.resolveEffective(...)` for every change to *how hard* it's loaded. The two genuinely new features (monitoring, limiter diagnosis) need one new pure-function seam each, because they read training history and benchmarks rather than a single day's context — deliberately kept separate rather than forced into the existing two.

## User Stories

### G1 — Progression

1. As the athlete, I want every hybrid-mode adaptation (aerobic base, limit bouldering, threshold work) to land at full weekly frequency instead of fortnightly, so that six months of training actually produces six months of adaptation instead of three.
2. As the athlete, I want Base boulder-flavored Saturdays to run a genuinely sub-threshold capacity session instead of the same anaerobic-capacity triples Build uses, so that Base actually trains the aerobic-base quality it's named for and Build is a real progression, not a repeat.
3. As the athlete, I want the app to read my stored `sportGrade`/`boulderGrade`/`dominantStyle`/`dominantAngle` and surface a "likely limiter" readout, so that I'm not spending three sessions a week reinforcing a quality that isn't actually holding my grade back.
4. As the athlete, I want Base and Build sessions to include an explicit anti-style prompt (e.g. "include 2 anti-style problems") derived from my stored dominant style/angle, so that I don't unconsciously reinforce the same movement pattern every cycle.
5. As the athlete, I want a written end-of-cycle review checklist (which protocols to rotate, when to shift phase ratios toward my evolving limiter, when to ratchet Peak back up), so that cycle N+1 isn't byte-identical to cycle N with new kg numbers.
6. As the athlete, I want the retest week's Thursday and Saturday sessions to carry the same volume cut every other deload week gets, so that my benchmarks are measured fresh instead of at the end of an unbroken loading block, and so Build doesn't start on accumulated fatigue.
7. As the athlete, I want the app to warn me, in the UI, that a Sunday session lasting a long time can compromise Monday's max-hang work, so that I don't accidentally sabotage my heaviest finger session of the week.

### G2 — Peaking

8. As the athlete, I want a comp-format Peak Saturday (unseen problems, timed rounds, no beta rehearsal) when `peakType` is `'comp'`, so that I arrive at a competition tactically prepared, not just physically peaked.
9. As the athlete, I want comp-taper Thursdays to be short onsight-style bursts instead of the standard project/redpoint template, so that the specificity of the final two weeks matches the actual demand profile of a comp day.
10. As the athlete, I want the taper's authored session templates and the volume-cut logic to apply exactly once, not twice, so that my final quality sessions (e.g. redpoint goes) aren't silently cut below what the plan itself argues for.
11. As the athlete, I want a mid-cycle or end-of-taper micro-retest, so that Build/Peak percentages aren't still keyed off a 7+-week-old benchmark by the time I actually need them to be accurate.

### G3 — Durability

12. As the athlete, I want the antagonist/shoulder S&C block to run twice a week instead of once, and to never be fully dropped on deload weeks, so that the single highest residual injury risk in the plan (elbow tendinopathy from unopposed pulling volume) gets consistently addressed.
13. As the athlete, I want the negative-benchmark (assisted-hang) branch of the load calculator to be clamped so it can never prescribe *less* assistance than my tested maximum, so that an edge case in the math can never silently generate a supra-max prescription labelled "intro, low-end."
14. As the athlete, I want the app to track 3–5 concrete monitoring signals (readiness trending down over multiple weeks, RPE drifting up at a constant load, retests coming back flat or worse) with defined thresholds and responses, so that overreach gets caught before it becomes a tweak or an injury, not after.
15. As the athlete, I want low-readiness days to scale climbing session targets (sets/problems/minutes) and cap RPE, not just suppress hangboard kg, so that a "lighter" day is actually lighter across the whole session, including the highest-intensity climbing work.
16. As the athlete, I want the readiness/RPE autoregulation constants to stay documented as an app convention (not a cited finding) until my own logged data (via the monitoring model) gives a basis to tune them, so that the plan doesn't overstate the evidence behind numbers that were invented for this app.
17. As the athlete, I want a short reference note on converting Peak/Taper weeks for an outdoor trip (rock-type specificity, skin management, sequencing performance goals before endurance goals), so that the existing `trip` peak type is actually usable when the goal is outdoors.

## Implementation Decisions

Split into two groups, per the advisor review of this document's own drafting process: fabricating implementation decisions for gaps whose own verdict says "adjudicate first" would misrepresent them as settled. Group 1 has a concrete, already-recorded verdict and is ready for a ticket today. Group 2 needs its own short ADR or mini-spec before a ticket can be written — this document states the problem and the direction, not the internals.

### Group 1 — Spec-ready (verdict recorded and **owner-ratified 2026-07-15**, [wayfinder ratification ticket #34](https://github.com/slm37102/simple-climbing-training-planner/issues/34))

The picks embedded below were confirmed item-by-item by the owner — they are decisions, no longer drafting suggestions.

- **KG-B10 (retest-week pseudo-deload).** `Program`'s session-assembly logic currently gates the deload volume cut on the week-level `deload && !retest` flags, which exempts the *entire* retest week including Thursday/Saturday. Change the gate to be slot-scoped: Thursday/Saturday of a retest week get the normal deload volume cut applied (they have no "own structure" the way Monday's retest session does); Monday keeps the retest session as-is (it's a test protocol, not a volume-cuttable template).
- **KG-B11a (negative-benchmark safety clamp — the safety-bug half only, not the intensity-convention question below).** The load calculator's percentage-of-added-load math computes a range by multiplying the benchmark by a percentage band; for a negative benchmark (assisted hangs) this can currently produce a range that requires *less* assistance than the athlete's tested maximum — a supra-max prescription. Clamp both ends of the computed range so neither bound ever represents less assistance than the benchmark itself (i.e., the prescribed range can equal the tested maximum but never exceed it in the harder direction). This is a safety fix independent of the broader intensity-convention question (W12, listed in Group 2 below) and should ship regardless of how that's eventually resolved.
- **KG-B12 (Base boulder-Saturday intensity mismatch).** `Program`'s Saturday session builder currently serves the identical anaerobic-capacity triples/4×4 template to both Base and Build boulder-flavored weeks. Give Base boulder Saturdays their own session — **ratified pick: a high-volume flash pyramid** (~15–20 problems well below max, pyramiding through 2–3 grades, RPE 6–7.5; countable target per the gym-ready format, practical on commercial sets) — mirroring exactly the precedent already shipped for the sport-side equivalent (KG-B9). Label it `energySystem: 'Aerobic capacity'` so it picks up the ADR-0009 Base volume ramp automatically. Keep the existing triples/4×4 as the Build-only session, where its density progression already lives. No changes needed to the Base aerobic volume ramp (ADR-0009) — it already gates on `energySystem` starting with "Aerobic", so a correctly-labelled new session picks up the ramp automatically.
- **KG-B13 (taper double-cut).** Taper session templates are already authored at reduced volume, and the taper volume-cut logic scales them down a second time (e.g. authored 2×2 pull-ups ship as 1×2; authored "2–3 quality goes" ships as a target of 1). Ratified resolution: author taper templates at full (pre-taper) volume and let the existing taper volume-cut logic be the single source of the reduction — this matches how every other phase's templates already work (full volume, cut applied once by a separate mechanism) and needs no new exemption flag. Fix the resulting "1 goes" pluralization in the same pass.
- **KG-A13 (comp peak-format specificity).** The KG verdict's condition ("if comps are ever a real goal") is satisfied — the owner confirmed comps are a real peaking goal (ratification, 2026-07-15). `peakType === 'comp'` currently changes only taper length. Add a comp-specific Peak Saturday template (e.g. 2 rounds × 4 unseen problems, 4 minutes on / 4 off, flash-only — no beta rehearsal) and a comp-specific taper Thursday template (short onsight-style bursts on unfamiliar problems), both gated on `peakType === 'comp'`; `trip`/`project` peaks keep the existing project/redpoint templates unchanged.
- **KG-A7-dosing (antagonist work frequency — the dosing half of KG-A7 only; the return-from-tweak protocol is Group 2/out of scope, see below).** The antagonist/shoulder S&C block currently attaches only to Monday's session and is dropped entirely when that week is a deload. Add a second, shorter antagonist/shoulder dose to Tuesday's light-day session (the coach-review recommendation is a ~10-minute block), and remove the `!isDeload` guard on Monday's block so it's reduced in volume on deload weeks (consistent with how every other exercise handles deloads) rather than dropped to zero.
- **KG-A10 (style-biased session text).** `dominantStyle`/`dominantAngle` are collected in global benchmarks but read by no prescription path. Add a text-only bias: Base/Build boulder-flavored Thursday/Saturday prescriptions append an explicit anti-style cue (e.g. "include 2 anti-style problems") derived from the stored values. No new UI, no schema change, no automated problem selection — the athlete still picks their own boulders; this only changes the prescription text they read before doing so. Full auto-programming stays out of scope, matching the recorded verdict.
- **A8-checklist (written end-of-cycle review, the doc-only deliverable of KG-A8).** Author a short, reusable checklist (in `docs/`, not code) that names what to review at the end of each cycle: which protocols to rotate, when phase ratios should shift toward the athlete's current limiter, when Peak aggressiveness should ratchet back up. This is a content-authoring task, not a code change — it can proceed without a design ADR because its own verdict already scoped it as "a written checklist first, automation later."
- **G2-outdoor-note (KG-A11's accepted mitigation).** Author the half-page "converting Peak/Taper weeks for a trip" reference note the gap's own resolution calls for (rock-type specificity, skin management, RCTM's performance-before-endurance sequencing). Documentation only, already accepted as the closing mitigation — not a design question.
- **Coach-§8 Sunday/Monday finger-stacking hint (untracked recovery finding — not related to KG-B10).** Surface a UI hint (or cap) when a long optional Sunday session immediately precedes a Build/Peak Monday max-hang session. Small, self-contained; the coach review offered a cap or a warning — **ratified pick: warning-only** (a Today-tab hint on Sundays preceding a Build/Peak Monday, prescription untouched), preserving athlete agency on the optional day.

### Group 2 — Needs its own ADR/mini-spec before a ticket can be written

- **W4 / hybrid flavor-alternation dilution (root cause of KG-B4 and part of the case for KG-B12).** The coach review names two directions — mix specific stimuli *within* the week instead of alternating whole weeks, or push hybrid athletes through boulder-focus/sport-focus *cycles* back-to-back using the double-block machinery that already exists — and explicitly says this needs a design decision before it's spec-ready, not just an ADR restating the problem. **This document does not choose between them.** Whichever direction is chosen has different session-slot implications (within-week mixing needs new slot logic; per-cycle focus needs onboarding/settings changes to let the athlete declare a focus block) — that's exactly why it needs to be decided first, on paper, before any code-level spec is written.
- **KG-A4 (monitoring model).** The gap's own verdict is explicit: "define 3–5 signals + thresholds + responses on paper first." User story 14 above names the shape (readiness trend, RPE drift, flat retests) but the actual thresholds, lookback windows, and response actions are a coaching judgment call this document is not positioned to make. Once decided, the natural implementation seam is a new pure function reading `Storage.listDays(...)`/benchmark history and returning a small set of flagged signals — kept separate from `Program.build`/`Loads.resolveEffective` because it reads history, not a single day's context.
- **KG-A1/KG-D2 (limiter-diagnosis readout).** The norms table this needs already exists (`benchmark-norms.md`, KG-C6 closed) and user story 3 above names the desired outcome, but KG-C6's own closing note flags that within this athlete's tier, finger strength explains only ~17% of grade variance and pulling ~8–12% — meaning the actual decision rule for what counts as "likely limiter" (and how prominently to surface a readout this statistically weak) is a judgment call that hasn't been made, not an engineering detail. Needs a short ADR before implementation, explicitly scoped small per the existing recommendation.
- **KG-A7-return-from-tweak (the non-dosing half of KG-A7).** "Finger tweaked but not injured" is the most common intermediate scenario and has no protocol today. The gap's own verdict says this is "research-first, possibly a printed reference rather than app logic" — it may never become a code change at all. Out of this document's scope until that research happens.
- **KG-A14 / W5 — readiness gating for climbing sessions (coach top-10 #7 — user story 15).** Today the readiness multiplier only feeds `Loads.resolveEffective`, which only computes kg for `hangboard`/`pullup`; the highest-injury climbing sessions (30/30 lactic at RPE 9.5–10, limit bouldering, 4×4s) get zero readiness modulation. The coach direction is concrete — on low readiness scale climbing `prescribedTarget` by the same multiplier and cap RPE; on suggest-rest surface a session *swap*, not just a kg suppression — but it introduces genuinely new prescription-mutation semantics (an RPE cap on climbing kinds; a substitution rule like 30/30 → 60/60; what "swap" means on a suggest-rest day) that aren't a recorded verdict. Needs a short design decision picking the substitution/cap rules before it's ticketed. Natural seam: the same `prescribeForContext`/readiness path, extended to climbing kinds — but the *rules* come first.
- **W13 / mid-cycle benchmark staleness (coach top-10 #9 — tracked inside KG-A8's entry as of 2026-07-15 — user story 11).** The only retest closes Base (week 6 of 12); Build gains are never captured and Peak's percentages ride week-6 numbers, and cycle N+1 starts from a 7+-week-old benchmark. The coach offers a two-way fork — a max-hang-only micro-retest folded into the first Build Monday's warm-up, *or* an end-of-taper (post-goal) retest slot — both feeding the existing `setGlobalBenchmarks` path. Small, but the fork is a real either/or (they land in different phases and answer different questions: mid-cycle keeps Build/Peak honest; end-of-cycle feeds inter-cycle progression per KG-A8), so it needs a one-line decision before ticketing rather than a fabricated pick here.
- **KG-B11 / W12 — the %-added-load intensity convention (coach top-10 #6, rated High — the broader question the Group 1 KG-B11a clamp is carved out of).** All hangboard/pull-up percentages scale *added* benchmark load, but physiological intensity tracks *total system* load (bodyweight + added), so Base's "intro 55–70%" hangs actually sit at ~90%+ of true max from week 1 while the plan reasons about the numbers as if they were %1RM. The coach names two directions — recompute prescriptions on total system load (`(bw + added) × pct − bw`), or keep the added-load convention but re-derive the phase percentages so true intensity genuinely ramps — plus a UI-tooltip disclosure of whichever convention is chosen. Either choice shifts every suggested load mid-plan (needs a migration note and probably a retest), which is exactly why it's a decision, not a spec detail. The **negative-benchmark safety clamp (KG-B11a) is Group 1 and ships independently** of this; this broader convention question stays here until adjudicated.

## Testing Decisions

**What makes a good test here:** assert on the externally observable output of the two dominant seams — the returned session/exercise shape from `Program.build(plan, dateISO)`, and the returned `{ suggestedKg, range, reason }` shape from `Loads.resolveEffective(...)` — for specific fixture dates/settings combinations. Do not assert on internal helper functions (e.g. whichever function ends up building the new Base-boulder-Saturday template) — those are free to be refactored as long as the seam's output is correct. This matches the existing pattern in `tests/index.html`, where every `[ADR-000N]`-tagged block is a flat list of assertions against these same two seams' return values for chosen fixture inputs, not against intermediate helpers.

- **Program.build seam** covers: KG-B10 (assert a retest week's Thursday/Saturday sessions carry the deload volume cut while Monday's session is the unmodified retest template), KG-B12 (assert Base boulder-flavor Saturdays return the new capacity session while Build boulder-flavor Saturdays still return the triples/4×4 session), KG-B13 (assert a taper week's pull-up/project sessions show the single, correctly-scaled volume, not a double cut), KG-A13 (assert Peak Saturday and taper Thursday differ correctly by `peakType`), KG-A7-dosing (assert Tuesday's session includes an antagonist/shoulder exercise, and assert Monday's antagonist block is present but volume-reduced rather than absent on a deload week), KG-A10 (assert the anti-style text appears in Base/Build boulder session prescriptions when a non-default `dominantStyle`/`dominantAngle` is set).
- **Loads.resolveEffective seam** covers: KG-B11a (assert that for a negative `maxHang20mm` benchmark, the returned `range` never requires less assistance than the benchmark itself, across the full prescribed percentage band).
- **New pure-function seam(s), once Group 2 items are adjudicated:** the monitoring model (KG-A4/KG-D6) and the limiter readout (KG-A1/KG-D2) should each get their own small, dedicated module exporting a pure function of stored history/benchmarks → signals or a limiter verdict — testable the same way (fixed fixture history in, asserted signal/verdict shape out), without needing to touch `Program` or `Loads`. This document does not name the module(s) or their exact shape, since the underlying decision hasn't been made (see Group 2).
- **Prior art:** every ADR-0004 through ADR-0009 closure added its own `[ADR-000N]`-tagged block to `tests/index.html` asserting exactly this way. This spec's tickets should follow the same convention — one new tagged block per closed gap (e.g. `[KG-B10]`, `[KG-B12]`, `[KG-A13]`) — and each closure bumps `sw.js`'s `CACHE` per the project's standing convention.

## Out of Scope

- **Publishing any of this to the issue tracker as a single `ready-for-agent` ticket.** This document is a roadmap, not an atomic feature; per the project's usual workflow it should be sliced into individually-scoped tickets (one per Group 1 item, or per adjudicated Group 2 item) before any tracker issue is created. No tracker issue has been created as part of producing this document.
- **All Group 2 items**, until their own ADR/mini-spec exists (see above).
- **Anything already marked Won't-fix or Accepted in `knowledge-gaps.md`**: KG-A11 (outdoor conversion — mitigated by the doc note above, not full outdoor-mode logic), KG-A12 (cross-session fatigue/ACWR modeling — folds into KG-A4's monitoring signals instead), KG-C2/KG-C3/KG-C4 (evidence gaps that would require running trials), KG-C7 (documenting, not replacing, the autoregulation constants), KG-C8 (a one-time audit already scoped separately).
- **Everything in `knowledge-gaps.md`'s own "Deliberately out of scope" list**: multi-user/product features, an AI-coach chat, wearables/HRV integration, nutrition planning, a full ACWR/fatigue model, an in-app technique curriculum beyond drill-of-the-week text, and automated style programming beyond the text-bias described in KG-A10 above.
- **Return-from-finger-tweak protocol** (the non-dosing half of KG-A7) — research-first, not a code deliverable in this pass.
- **Engineering-quality items** (sync robustness, accessibility, PWA correctness, refactors) — owned by `improvement-audit.md`, not this document, per the existing project convention.
- **Coach-review findings deliberately excluded from this pass** (named here so the omission is visible, not silent — several are legitimate but sit outside this document's goal-gap-closing remit or want their own decision):
  - **W6 (no lower-body / hip-power / fall-conditioning)** and **W7 (token core; narrow grip vocabulary — pinch/sloper/drag untrained)** — these are *exercise-selection* expansions, not gap closures, and each adds a new training quality (leg power, body-tension, grip diversity) with its own dose/injury trade-offs and equipment needs. They deserve their own scoping conversation (arguably one combined "S&C + grip breadth" spec) rather than being folded into this roadmap. W6's fall/landing-conditioning piece is also the §9 acute-injury gap — worth flagging as the highest-value of this cluster for the lead goal.
  - **W1 (Build only 3 wk at default length)** and **W3 (six loaded weeks into the goal)** — periodization-structure tuning. W3's cheapest fix *is* KG-B10 (already in Group 1); the residual idea (a half-deload at the Build→Peak boundary, or biasing the phase split toward Build) is a change to `buildPhasePattern`'s derived split that should be its own decision, not bundled here.
  - **W9 (campus placed after two maximal blocks)** and **W10 (muddled Base Thursday objective)** — Low/Low-Medium session-ordering and prescription-clarity polish; real but below this roadmap's threshold, appropriate as small standalone tickets.
  - **W14 layoff-decay-floor half** — the "Push"-stacking half of W14 was already closed by ADR-0009's +5%/session cap; the remaining half (extending decay below the ×0.85 floor after ~8 weeks off, or forcing a re-benchmark) is a Low-Medium edge-of-the-envelope tweak to `layoffDecay`, deferrable and best decided alongside the W13 retest question.

## Further Notes

- **Suggested sequencing:** Group 1 items are independent of each other and can be ticketed/built in any order; the coach review's own priority ranking (KG-B10/B11a as cheap-and-high-value, then KG-B12, then the rest) is a reasonable default if the athlete wants one. Group 2 items should be adjudicated (short ADR or grilling session) before they're ticketed at all — attempting to spec their internals now would mean inventing a design the athlete hasn't actually chosen.
- **Recommended next step:** run `/to-tickets` against this document's Group 1 list to produce individually-scoped, independently agent-implementable tickets, each tagged `ready-for-agent` in the tracker as usual. Group 2 items should go through a design pass first (the `grilling` or `domain-modeling` skill, or a short ADR) rather than straight to tickets.
- **Why this isn't tagged `ready-for-agent` as one issue:** the skill's own template assumes a single atomic feature; a multi-item roadmap (10 spec-ready + 7 decision-first) spanning multiple modules and several not-yet-decided design questions doesn't fit that shape, and tagging it as such would either produce an unreviewably large PR or force an agent to silently make the Group 2 design calls this document deliberately leaves open.
