# Converting Peak/Taper Weeks for an Outdoor Trip (KG-A11 mitigation)

The plan's Peak/Taper phases are written for a gym-based comp or a single redpoint-day goal. If your goal is a multi-day **outdoor trip**, use this checklist to adapt those weeks by hand — there is no code support for this (see [KG-A11](knowledge-gaps.md), Won't-fix).

## 1. Substitute sessions, don't just skip them
Once you're at the crag, outdoor mileage *replaces* the prescribed gym sessions in Peak/Taper — don't try to do both in the same week. Keep the **shape** (session count, rest-day placement, volume trending down) but swap "boulder/route session" line items for actual outdoor days. A full rest day still goes before every outdoor performance day, same as the gym prescription's rest-before-main-day pattern.

## 2. Match rock type and style before you leave
The plan has no style-individualization for outdoor rock (see [KG-A10](knowledge-gaps.md), open). In the last 1–2 gym weeks before the trip, bias session content toward the destination's dominant hold type and angle (crimpy vertical vs. steep/compression vs. slab) rather than following the generic prescription verbatim. This is a practical judgment call, not corpus-verified.

## 3. Manage skin around session spacing
Outdoor rock (especially granite/sandstone) is harder on skin than gym texture. Space hard outdoor days so skin recovers — don't stack two max-effort days back to back even if the training-day cadence would otherwise allow it, and treat visible skin thinning as a signal to insert an extra rest day regardless of what the plan's calendar says. This is practical guidance, not sourced from the verified corpus.

## 4. Sequence goals within the trip: powerful first, endurance later
This is corpus-verified: within RCTM's Performance Phase, the climber "targets more powerful/bouldery goal routes early and shifts toward more endurance-demanding and onsight objectives later" (`docs/research/verified-findings.md`, "Peaking & tapering for a goal" section, the RCTM/Cragmama entry, vote 3-0 — source quote: *"...You should attempt more powerful goal routes earlier in the Performance (PE) Phase, and more endurance-demanding goal …"* [truncated in source], illustrated by Cragmama's example: 5.12a/b sends in week 1, a pair of 5.12b's in week 2, five 5.11 onsight/flashes by week 3). For a multi-day trip, front-load your hardest/most powerful projects in the first days and save volume/endurance or onsight objectives for the tail end.

## 5. Taper into the trip, not through it
Hold intensity, cut volume, same as the app's existing taper logic — the difference is that once outdoors, "volume" means fewer/shorter outdoor sessions, not gym sets. Don't try to bank extra gym training in the final week to compensate for lost gym days at the crag.
